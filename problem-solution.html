Problem & Solution

How FLOW is tackling the water crisis with innovation and technology.

The Problem: Water & Sanitation Crisis

Over 2 billion people globally lack access to safely managed drinking water, while 4.2 billion struggle with inadequate sanitation services. This leads to severe health crises, economic challenges, and social inequality.

In Liberia, more than 45% of the population relies on unsafe water sources, leading to waterborne diseases like cholera and diarrhea. This issue disproportionately affects rural and low-income communities, who often spend hours collecting unsafe water from distant sources.

Global Statistic: "By 2025, half of the world's population will be living in water-stressed areas." (UNICEF)

Local Statistic: "In Liberia, only 10% of rural communities have access to clean drinking water." (WHO)

The Solution: FLOW’s Unique Approach

Solar-Powered Water Kiosks: FLOW’s solution leverages solar energy to power water extraction and filtration processes. This ensures uninterrupted water access, even in remote areas without reliable electricity.

IoT-Enabled Monitoring: Our kiosks are equipped with IoT sensors that track water quality and usage in real-time. This data is sent to a central dashboard, allowing for remote monitoring and management of the water systems.

Automated Water Distribution & Payment: Users can pay for water through mobile platforms like Orange Money. This automated system ensures accountability and affordability for all users.

Solar Energy Efficiency: "Solar energy can reduce water extraction costs by up to 30%." (World Bank)

IoT Monitoring Impact: "Real-time monitoring has been shown to reduce water system downtime by 40%." (IoT in WASH Report)

The Impact: FLOW’s Expected Outcomes

Direct Impact: FLOW’s kiosks will provide clean water to 50,000 people in underserved communities, reducing the incidence of waterborne diseases by 30%.

Economic & Social Benefits: Families will save 2 hours per day on water collection, freeing time for education and work. Our mobile payment system will promote a cashless economy and empower local entrepreneurs.

Health Improvements: "By improving water access, we aim to reduce waterborne diseases by 30-40%." (WHO)

Time Savings: "Families will save up to 65% of their daily time spent collecting water." (UN Water)

Why Invest in FLOW?

FLOW is addressing one of the world’s most critical challenges: access to clean water. With our innovative solar-powered kiosks, IoT-enabled monitoring, and automated payment system, we are set to transform water access in underserved communities.

Investing in FLOW means contributing to health improvements, economic empowerment, and a future where clean water is accessible to all.
